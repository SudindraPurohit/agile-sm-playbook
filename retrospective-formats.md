# Retrospective Templates

> **Facilitator note:** Pick the format based on team mood and sprint context. Use Format 1 (Start/Stop/Continue) for new teams or after a rough sprint — it's the safest and most structured. Use Format 3 (Sailboat) when the team needs to reconnect with goals. Timebox strictly: 60 minutes maximum for a 2-week sprint.

---

## Format 1: Start / Stop / Continue

**Best for:** Teams new to retros, or after a difficult sprint
**Time:** 45–60 minutes
**Participants:** Scrum team + Product Owner (optional)

### Structure

| Phase | Time | Activity |
|-------|------|----------|
| Set the stage | 5 min | SM reads the Prime Directive: *"Regardless of what we discover, we understand and truly believe that everyone did the best job they could, given what they knew at the time."* |
| Generate insights | 15 min | Silent sticky note writing — one idea per sticky across three columns: START / STOP / CONTINUE |
| Gather data | 10 min | Group similar stickies, team dot-votes on top 3 per column |
| Decide actions | 20 min | For each top-voted item: assign an owner, set a due date, add to next sprint backlog if needed |
| Close | 5 min | Retro on the retro — one word each to describe how this session felt |

### Facilitator tips
- If the team is quiet during silent writing, play low background music
- Never let one person dominate the STOP column — anonymise stickies if needed
- Every action item must have a named owner. "The team will..." is not an owner.

## Format 2: 4Ls — Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For

**Best for:** Mid-project check-ins, teams that have been running retros for 3+ sprints
**Time:** 50–60 minutes

### Structure

| Column | Prompt |
|--------|--------|
| **Liked** | What went well that we should protect? |
| **Learned** | What did we discover — about our process, tech, or each other? |
| **Lacked** | What was missing that would have helped us? |
| **Longed For** | What do we wish we had — tools, clarity, support? |

### Facilitator tips
- "Lacked" and "Longed For" often overlap — that's fine, let the team place stickies where they feel right
- Focus action planning on "Lacked" — it's the most actionable column
- "Learned" is often skipped in action planning but is valuable for team knowledge documentation

## Format 3: Sailboat (Speed Boat Variant)

**Best for:** Teams losing sight of goals, or when motivation needs a reset
**Time:** 50–60 minutes

### Visual setup
Draw a sailboat on the whiteboard (or Miro):
- **Wind** (pushing us forward) = what's helping the team
- **Anchors** (slowing us down) = impediments and friction
- **Rocks ahead** (risks) = things we can see coming that worry us
- **Island** (destination) = what does a great sprint / quarter look like?

### Structure
1. Team silently adds stickies to each area (10 min)
2. SM reads out anchors first — these become impediment candidates
3. Team votes on which rocks to address this sprint
4. Actions assigned with owners

### Facilitator tips
- Start with the Island — remind the team what they're sailing toward before discussing anchors
- Rocks are often ignored. Push the team: "If this risk hits, what does that cost us?"

## Format 4: Mad / Sad / Glad

**Best for:** After a high-stress sprint, or when team morale needs attention
**Time:** 40–50 minutes

### Structure
Three columns: **Mad** | **Sad** | **Glad**

- Mad = things that frustrated or blocked us
- Sad = things we didn't achieve or lost
- Glad = things that made us proud or happy

### Facilitator tips
- This format surfaces emotions more directly than others — be prepared to hold space
- Do not jump to action planning immediately. Spend 10 minutes just acknowledging what's in the Mad and Sad columns before moving to solutions
- This is not a blame session. If things get personal, redirect to process: "What about the *process* caused this?"

---

## Format 5: DAKI — Drop, Add, Keep, Improve

**Best for:** Mature teams, process-heavy sprints, or after a PI
**Time:** 50–60 minutes

| Column | Question |
|--------|----------|
| **Drop** | What are we doing that adds no value? |
| **Add** | What should we introduce that we're not doing? |
| **Keep** | What's working well that we must protect? |
| **Improve** | What are we doing that could be done better? |

### Facilitator tips
- "Drop" is the most valuable column — teams rarely give themselves permission to stop doing things
- Push for specificity: "Drop daily standups" is not useful. "Drop the last 10 minutes of standup where we talk about things that aren't blockers" is.
- Every "Improve" item needs a concrete first step, not just an intention

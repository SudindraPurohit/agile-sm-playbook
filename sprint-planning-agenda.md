# Sprint Planning Agenda Template

> **Facilitator note:** Sprint Planning is timeboxed to 2 hours per week of sprint (4 hours for a 2-week sprint). The SM owns the clock, not the content. Your job is to keep the team moving, surface blockers early, and ensure the sprint goal is clear before anyone commits to a single story.

---

## Pre-Planning Checklist (SM completes 24hrs before)

- [ ] Backlog is refined — top 1.5x sprint capacity worth of stories are estimated and ready
- [ ] Definition of Ready confirmed for all candidate stories
- [ ] Team capacity calculated (accounts for leave, meetings, part-time members)
- [ ] Previous sprint velocity noted as reference point
- [ ] Any known dependencies or external blockers flagged to PO

---

## Agenda (4 hours for 2-week sprint)

### Part 1: WHAT will we do? (2 hours)
**Owner: Product Owner leads, SM facilitates**

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0:00–0:10 | SM opens: review sprint goal from last time, confirm team capacity |
| 0:10–0:30 | PO presents top backlog items — context, business value, acceptance criteria |
| 0:30–1:30 | Team selects stories, asks clarifying questions, confirms understanding |
| 1:30–2:00 | Team and PO agree on Sprint Goal — one sentence that describes the value delivered |

**Sprint Goal format:**
> *"By the end of this sprint, we will [deliver X] so that [business outcome Y]."*

---

### Part 2: HOW will we do it? (2 hours)
**Owner: Development Team leads, SM facilitates**

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 2:00–2:15 | Team breaks stories into tasks (SM tracks on board) |
| 2:15–3:30 | Task estimation, dependency identification, capacity check |
| 3:30–3:45 | Identify risks and impediments — SM logs these immediately |
| 3:45–4:00 | Final commitment — team confirms sprint backlog, SM confirms DoR met for all items |

---

## During Planning — SM Watchpoints

**Scope creep signals:**
- PO adding stories after Part 1 closes → redirect: "That goes into the backlog for next sprint"
- Team gold-plating tasks → "Does this task directly support the sprint goal?"

**Capacity red flags:**
- Team committing to more than 80% of theoretical capacity → push back
- Key dependency not yet resolved → flag as impediment before commitment

**Sprint Goal quality check:**
- If the team can't state the sprint goal in one sentence, planning isn't done
- If the goal is "complete all stories in the backlog", that's not a goal

---

## Post-Planning (SM completes within 1 hour)

- [ ] Sprint backlog updated in Jira with all tasks and owners
- [ ] Sprint goal documented and visible on team board
- [ ] Impediment log updated with anything raised during planning
- [ ] Capacity and velocity noted for team records
- [ ] Any dependencies flagged to relevant teams or stakeholders
